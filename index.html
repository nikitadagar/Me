About

I'm currently a Computer Science grad student at the University of Florida.
This summer I interned at Royal Bank of Canada's innovation lab in Orlando, FL where I worked on designing and developing enrollment of users into RBC's banking services through mobile.
At UF, I was a part of the Intelligent Natural Interaction Technology lab advised by Dr. Lisa Anthony where I explored how to motivate children to be more active through video games, using full body gesture recognition such as Microsoft Kinect. Previously, I worked at Digital Green as an Assistant Software Engineer and got my bachelor's degree at IIIT - Delhi.
I love building technology that makes people's lives easier. I'm particularly interested in Human Computer Interaction and Natural User Interfaces.

Research

Kinect games for exercise (Ongoing)

The INIT Lab (the Intelligent Natural Interaction Technology Laboratory) is directed by Dr. Lisa Anthony, and is part of the CISE Department at the University of Florida. The INIT Lab focuses on advanced interaction technologies such as touch, speech, and gesture, especially for children.
I am exploring how we can motivate children to be more active through video games, using full body gesture recognition with Microsoft Kinect. I am currently developing a WPF application which recognizes body gestures like Jumping Jacks, Wave etc. You can see it work here. or check out the INIT website.

A Study of Cyber-Security, Personality Traits and Culture - U.S., India and Sarjah, 2013

Our study looks at the relationship between secure behavior online, the ability of users to handle security events and the correlation to personality traits. The questions we attempted to answer are the following:

  • Are certain personality traits linked to less secure behavior?
  • Are certain personality traits linked to higher ability to handle security-related events?
  • How does culture affect secure behavior and our ability to handle security-related events?
